About

Yuemin He is a writer, translator, and editor. Her book chapters, essays, short stories, and book reviews appear in The Emergence of Buddhist American Literature, Religion and the Arts, Chinese Translators Journal, Inquiry, Yuan Yang, The Northern Virginia Review, and Cha: An Asian Literary Journal. Her essay “Bundle the Anthology: Teaching Asian North American Literature in a Community College Classroom” is forthcoming in Teaching Approaches to Asian American Literature (MLA). Her poetry translations, between Chinese and English have been anthologized in Oxford Anthology of Modern and Contemporary American Poetry (2nd ed., 2015) and have appeared in Metamorphoses, Ezra, The Northern Virginia Review, The Cincinnati Review, Exchanges, 91st Meridian, and other sites. She is co-chair of Pen Rose Triangle Translation, a bilingual WeChat post site. She was a recipient of 2011 National Endowment of Humanities Summer Institute Fellowship as well as the 2013 Japan Study Association Workshop Fellowship along with many college awards for her scholarly and creative engagement. She holds a PhD in English Language and Literature, and is currently an English professor at Northern Virginia Community College.





Yuemin was born in Chengdu, China. More than two decades ago, she came to the United States to study for her PhD degree in Illinois, and then settled down on the East Coast. Though her last name is HE, she is a female, not a male. Her last name is pronounced as He, close to Ho, rather than Hee. She likes reading and writing. In her spare time, she watches movies, travels, gardens, and cooks. Her Sichuan dishes are fantastic, and her dog is speedy.
